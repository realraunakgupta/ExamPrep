Top 3 Most Likely Topics & Solutions

Topic 1

Bayes' Theorem

Common Pattern:

"A factory has 2/3 machines producing parts. X% are defective. A part is chosen randomly and is defective. Find the probability it came from machine Y."

Generic Solution Logic:
  1. Define Prior Events: Let E₁, E₂ be the machines. Define their probabilities P(E₁), P(E₂).
  2. Define Outcome Event: Let A = "Item is defective".
  3. Find Conditionals: Identify P(A|E₁) and P(A|E₂) from the text.
  4. Apply Formula:
    P(E₁|A) = [P(E₁)·P(A|E₁)] / [Σ P(Eᵢ)·P(A|Eᵢ)]
Topic 2

Normal Distribution

Common Pattern:

"Items are normally distributed with mean μ and variance σ². Find percentage of items having value > X or between X and Y."

Generic Solution Logic:
  1. Identify Parameters: Extract μ (mean) and σ (standard deviation). Careful: if variance is given, root it for σ.
  2. Standardize to Z: Convert the target value X to Z-score:
    Z = (X - μ) / σ
  3. Lookup & Calculate: Use the provided Z-table values (e.g., P(0<z<1)=0.3413) to find the area under the curve. Remember total area = 1, half = 0.5.
Topic 3

PDFs & Constant 'K'

Common Pattern:

"Given probability density function f(x,y) = K(x+y) for 0<x<1. Find K, and find P(X < 0.5)."

Generic Solution Logic:
  1. The Core Axiom: The total probability over the entire domain must equal exactly 1.
  2. Integrate/Sum: Set up the double integral (for joint continuous) or single integral:
    ∫ ∫ f(x,y) dx dy = 1
  3. Solve for K: Evaluate the integral with the given limits, equate to 1, and isolate K algebraically.

Process Flow: Solving Bayes' Theorem (Exam Strategy)

Step 1
Identify all 'Base' Events (e.g. Machines A, B) & calculate P(Eᵢ)
➜
Step 2
Identify the 'Observed' Event (e.g. Defective Item)
➜
Step 3
Extract Conditional Probabilities P(Observed|Base)
➜
Step 4
Multiply branches & Divide specific branch by total sum.

B.Tech Programmes

Subject: Probability, Statistics and Linear programming

Time: 1.5 Hrs. Max. Marks: 30

Note: Attempt Q. No. 1 which is compulsory and any two more questions from the remaining.

Q1. Attempt all parts: (4 x 2.5 = 10 Marks)

(a) If a random variable X has the PDF f(x) = cx² for 0 ≤ x ≤ 2 and 0 otherwise. Find the value of constant 'c'.

(b) X is a Poisson variable and it is found that the probability at X=2 is twice the probability at X=1. Find the probability at x=0.

(c) Briefly define a probability space with a relevant engineering example.

(d) A die is thrown twice and the sum of the numbers appearing is observed to be 7. What is the conditional probability that the number 3 has appeared at least once?

Q2. (10 Marks)

A manufacturing company has three plants A, B, and C. Plant A produces 40% of the company's output, Plant B produces 35%, and Plant C produces 25%. Records show that 2% of the items produced by A, 3% of items produced by B, and 4% of items produced by C are defective.

If an item is drawn at random from the total output and is found to be defective, find the probability that it was produced by Plant B.

Q3. (10 Marks)

A sample of 500 electronic components was tested to find their lifespan. The results produced a mean lifespan of μ = 15 hours and a variance of 4 hours. Assuming the data is normally distributed, determine the number of components expected to have a lifespan of:

  • More than 18 hours
  • Between 13 and 17 hours

(Given that P(0 < z < 1.5) = 0.4332, P(0 < z < 1) = 0.3413)

Mid Term Examination

B.Tech Programmes (Under the Aegis of USICT)

Semester: Third / Fourth  |  Paper Code: BS-202

Subject: Probability, Statistics and Linear Programming

Time: 1½ Hrs. Max. Marks: 30

Note: Attempt Q. No. 1 which is compulsory and any two more questions from the remaining. Only scientific calculators are allowed.

Q1. Answer the following compulsory questions: (4 × 2.5 = 10 Marks)

(a) In a highly automated manufacturing process, the historical probability of producing a defective sensor is 0.005. If these sensors are packaged and shipped in boxes of 400 units, utilize the Poisson approximation of the Binomial distribution to calculate the precise probability that a randomly selected shipment box contains exactly one defective sensor. (Given e-2 = 0.1353)

(b) Determine the specific mathematical constant K such that the function f(x, y) = K(x² + y²) defined strictly within the continuous bounds 0 < x < 1 and 0 < y < 1 serves as a valid joint probability density function.

(c) For a given discrete Binomial distribution, mathematical analysis reveals that the mean evaluates to 4 and the statistical variance evaluates to 1.2. Determine the fundamental parameters n (number of trials) and p (probability of success) of this distribution. Subsequently, calculate the exact probability P(X = 0).

(d) Define the Moment Generating Function (MGF) mathematically. State its primary utility in advanced statistical analysis, and formally prove that the first derivative of the MGF with respect to t, when evaluated exactly at t=0, yields the mathematical expectation (mean) of the distribution.

Q2. Attempt the following: (5 + 5 = 10 Marks)

(a) A corporate IT infrastructure relies on three primary servers (A, B, and C) which process 40%, 35%, and 25% of the total internal network traffic, respectively. Extensive diagnostic logs indicate that the probability a data packet is catastrophically dropped by Server A is 2%, by Server B is 3%, and by Server C is 5%. A network engineer operating at an endpoint discovers a dropped packet. Utilizing Bayes' Theorem, calculate the exact probability that this specific dropped packet was processed and subsequently dropped by Server B.

(b) A continuous random variable, denoted as X, possesses a probability density function defined by the following piecewise polynomial expression:
f(x) = C(4x - 2x²) for 0 < x < 2
f(x) = 0 otherwise
Execute the following computations:
  • Determine the required value of the constant C to validate the PDF.
  • Calculate the Expected Value, E[X], of the distribution.
  • Calculate the specific geometric probability P(X > 1).
Q3. Attempt the following: (5 + 5 = 10 Marks)

(a) The joint probability distribution mapping two discrete random variables, X and Y, is provided by the following statistical matrix:

X ↓ \ Y → 1 2 3
0 0.10 0.15 0.05
1 0.20 0.30 0.20

Based on this matrix, systematically compute the following:

  • The independent marginal probability distributions of X and Y.
  • The conditional probability distribution of X, strictly given that Y=2.
  • The conditional expectation of X, given Y=2.

(b) The operational lifespan of a specific industrial-grade electronic relay is established to be normally distributed, possessing a population mean of 500 hours and a standard deviation of 40 hours. Calculate the precise probability that a randomly selected relay drawn from the production line will exhibit an operational lifespan:

  • Falling strictly between 460 hours and 560 hours.
  • Exceeding 580 hours.

(Given reference values: P(0 ≤ Z ≤ 1.0) = 0.3413, P(0 ≤ Z ≤ 1.5) = 0.4332, P(0 ≤ Z ≤ 2.0) = 0.4772)

Q4. Attempt the following: (5 + 5 = 10 Marks)

(a) An engineering study investigating the yield of a complex chemical process (Y) in direct relation to its operating temperature (X) records data across six independent experimental trials. The findings are as follows:

Variable X (Temperature) 10 15 20 25 30 35
Variable Y (Chemical Yield) 12 18 25 23 35 40

Construct the necessary statistical tables to calculate Karl Pearson's Coefficient of Correlation between the operating temperature and the chemical yield. Briefly interpret the mathematical nature of the derived relationship.

(b) An actuarial department within an insurance company models the monetary value of claims utilizing an Exponential Distribution defined by the probability density function f(x) = (1/5)e-x/5 for values where x > 0 (with x measured in thousands of dollars). Formulate the explicit moment generating function, MX(t), for this continuous distribution. Utilizing the properties of the derived MGF, mathematically calculate the variance of the insurance claims.
